Fresh Southern Peach Cobbler

Description:

Classic peach cobbler is the best when peaches are at their peak. This recipe in particular has over 3,000 five-star ratings. A warm mixture of sweetened, juicy peaches is topped with a biscuit-like dough and sprinkled in cinnamon sugar. Although it's made from scratch with fresh peaches, this easy peach cobbler is incredibly simple and comes together in just one hour.

What is peach cobbler you ask? A cobbler is a deep-dish fruit dessert with a sweetened fruit filling that is topped off with a biscuit-like dough. The dough may cover the entire dish or be dropped by the spoonful, earning it the name "cobbler" for its resemblance to a cobblestone street. It is the same fruit-and-pastry combo in pie, but without all the effort!

Ingredients:

  • 8 fresh peaches - peeled, pitted and sliced into thin wedges
  • 0.5 cup white sugar
  • 0.5 cup brown sugar
  • 0.25 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 0.125 teaspoon ground nutmeg
  • 1 teaspoon fresh lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons cornstarch
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon baking powder
  • 0.5 teaspoon salt
  • 6 tablespoons unsalted butter, chilled and cut into small pieces
  • 0.25 cup boiling water

Steps:

  1. Mix together: 3 tablespoons white sugar, 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  2. Preheat oven to 425 degrees F (220 degrees C)
  3. In a large bowl, combine peaches, quarter cup white sugar, quarter cup brown sugar, quarter teaspoon cinnamon, nutmeg, lemon juice, and cornstarch. Toss to coat evenly, and pour into a 2 quart baking dish. Bake in preheated oven for 10 minutes.
  4. Meanwhile, in a large bowl, combine flour, quarter cup white sugar, quarter cup brown sugar, baking powder, and salt. Blend in butter with your fingertips, or a pastry blender, until mixture resembles coarse meal. Stir in water until just combined.
  5. Remove peaches from oven, and drop spoonfuls of topping over them. Sprinkle entire cobbler with the sugar and cinnamon mixture. Bake until topping is golden, about 30 minutes.
